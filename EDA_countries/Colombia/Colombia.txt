Colombia experienced an economic boom in 1990 due its liberal economic policies and went from $120 billion in 1990 to $750 billion in 2010. Spending increased in 1990s and resulted in a large per captia increase in the last decade.

Males had higher enrollment in primary school than females where as at the same time enrollement in secondary and tertiary schools were almost similar with a constant increase after 2002. Again about 80% women are employed in the service sector with very little women employed in the agriculture sector. Employment in the agro sector for males increased sharply in 2000 and thus dropped in the other sectors, majorly service.
