Initial Analysis of Bangladesh show us that the Government spending has consistantly increased since 1970s with a sharp spike and subsequent decline a little before 1990.
GDP per capita has increased similarly through the past 50 years with a higher rate of increase in the past decade.
A higher number of females are employed in the Agriculture and the Industry sectors with Agriculture being the biggest employer of about 80% females and 50% males.
Industrial and Service sectors employ the remaining 20% females and Service sector employement accounts for about 40% males.
School enrollement in males is slightly higher than females with about 90,00,000 male enrollment and 89,00,000 in females in 2004.

Note: The sharp decrease in women employed in the industry sector and incease in the agriculture sector might be the reason for the spike in govt. spending before 1990.
